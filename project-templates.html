Project templates

Don’t start each new project from scratch

Reduce the amount of time

Reduce the amount of time you spend getting started on recurring projects by creating templates from current projects. Not only do you save time, but you capture and modify successful workflows that you know work well.

Build up a reusable project templates library and give your planners access to the template they need.

Decide which parts of a project you want to be copied (and which parts you want to leave behind)

Archive the original project after copying. And, you can add users, adjust anything you need before launching it again.

Keep users assigned to specific projects or remove them to start fresh.

Users are only notified when you adjust assignees and dates in archived projects.

Move projects around in project groups.

Adjust any dates while copying.

When the scope of a project changes, you can add task lists to running projects from your templates and shift dates forward/backward for not completed tasks to reprioritize.

Go beyond project templates

Go beyond project templates

Take the convenience of templates beyond the projects itself and stop manually copying chunks of projects. With Freedcamp you can:

Use copy move to move tasks (and subtasks) and task lists between your projects. You can copy everything, as is, or modify by removing assignees and due dates.

Add similar tasks to a set of projects, like end of financial year tasks for each customer of your accounting business (for example).

Import full sets of tasks from Excel to many projects at once.
